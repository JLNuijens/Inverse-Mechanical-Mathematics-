Inverse Mechanical Mathematics is a foundational, deterministic framework that maps the emergence of structure, multiplicity, and geometry directly from a single unit through a strict mechanical process of halving. Rather than relying on abstract variables or standard continuous mathematics, the system is entirely self-contained and driven solely by two operational indices: $  c  $ (the count) and $  d  $ (the depth).
Here is a breakdown of the core mechanics and conclusions established in the paper:
1. The Foundation: Axiom and Operation
Axiom of 1: The entire framework begins with a single, unbroken object:
$$1 = \sqrt{2^{0}}$$
This is the “axiom seat” labelled $  c=0  $, $  d=0  $.
Halving and Parity: The only allowed operation is partitioning (halving). Each halving step advances the count $  c  $ and unfolds new local depths $  d  $ on the exponent ladder $  \sqrt{2^{d}}  $.
Parity Rule: The reading at any local depth is strictly determined by the parity of the exponent $  d  $:

Even $  d  $: rational reading (no $  \sqrt{2}  $ factor).
Odd $  d  $: irrational reading carrying exactly one factor of $  \sqrt{2}  $.

2. The Chain Unfolding (Singular vs. Dual Counts)

Singular counts: Count 0 (axiom seat) owns only depth 0 (rational). Count 1 owns only depth 1 (irrational singular: $  \sqrt{2}  $, 2 domains).
Dual counts: From count 2 onward, each halving advances the exponent ladder by two whole numbers. Counts 2 through 5 therefore each own two consecutive local depths (one even + one odd). By the parity rule they carry both a rational reading and an irrational reading simultaneously.

3. The Two Operational Structures
The parity rule splits the chain into two parallel operational structures that grow in exact lockstep:

Square structure (rational): built from the even depths. At the terminal count $  c=5  $, $  d=8  $ it closes at $  32 \times 16 = \mathbf{512}  $.
Sphere structure (irrational): built from the odd depths. At the terminal count $  c=5  $, $  d=9  $ it closes at $  32 \times 16\sqrt{2} \approx \mathbf{724.077}  $.

$$5 \times 9 = 45.$$
Scaled along the binary ladder ($  45 \times 16  $) this produces the machined integer
$$\mathbf{5 \times 9 \times 16 = 720}$$

Between the pure machined values and their observed irrational twins there is a constant per-unit slip of exactly $  -0.005663  $, given in closed form by
$$-0.005663\ldots = \frac{32\sqrt{2}}{45} - 1.$$
This slip is not an error; it is the intrinsic differential (the “shape”) of the unity itself when the square and sphere are halved in lockstep at every count.
